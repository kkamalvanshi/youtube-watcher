- **Rule 1 — Track Everything That Matters**

  - **The math of fat loss**
    - 1 lb of fat = 3,500 calories
    - 500 cal/day deficit → 1 lb lost/week (500 × 7 = 3,500)
    - 1,000 cal/day deficit → 2 lbs lost/week
    - Eating "healthy" without tracking still causes fat gain — people do it constantly
    - Duke University study: subjects tracked calories with no diet or workout plan → lost an average of 5 lbs in 3 months just from awareness changing behavior

  - **Track calories burned — get a wearable**
    - Options: Oura Ring (Brandon's preference), Apple Watch, Garmin, Whoop
    - Brandon prefers Oura Ring because it blends into all attire including formal wear
    - Whoop looks odd with a suit; Apple Watch feels like a medical device; Samsung Watch is a joke
    - Devices are not 100% accurate but provide a workable range; can upload screenshots (e.g., Oura app) directly to Claude to correct discrepancies

  - **Track calories consumed — use Claude AI (or ChatGPT)**
    - Brandon used MyFitnessPal for 10+ years; now experimenting with Claude
    - Set up a long custom prompt in Claude (DM the word *tracking* to Brandon on Instagram to receive it)
    - Log food by typing what you ate or photographing the plate/label and uploading directly to the chat
    - Claude gives macros, calories, and a **running daily total**
    - Claude-exclusive feature: can connect to Apple Health data and display real-time caloric burn alongside intake → shows exact surplus or deficit at any moment
    - ChatGPT works similarly but can't pull Apple Health data natively; can still accept screenshot uploads
    - Weigh food when possible for accuracy; when not possible (e.g., catered events, restaurants), photograph the plate, describe what it is, and Claude estimates — tested against actual weights, within acceptable range
    - Sauces and cooked-in ingredients: just describe them verbally (e.g., "hibachi sauce") — easier than MyFitnessPal
    - Start a new session each day by telling Claude "new day" and it resets the running total

  - **Daily targets**
    - Calories: bodyweight (lbs) × 10 = daily calorie goal (200 lbs → 2,000 cal)
    - Protein: bodyweight (lbs) × 1 = daily grams of protein (200 lbs → 200g protein)
    - Beginners (first year of serious training): this approach burns fat AND builds muscle simultaneously
    - Experienced trainees: high protein preserves muscle during a cut
    - Brandon's own day example: 2,000 cal, 240g protein, carbs under 50g (keto), fat remainder

  - **Optional workout additions**
    - Diet is the most important factor; "you can't out-train a bad diet, but you can outdiet bad training"
    - To accelerate: 30–60 min steady-state cardio daily — walking, stationary bike, elliptical at conversational pace (not high-intensity)
    - Resistance training: 3–4 full-body workouts per week; 1–2 exercises per body part; 3–4 sets each
    - Kids and family can be used to burn extra calories (trampoline parks, water gun fights, walking)

- **Rule 2 — Stop Working on Bullshit; Focus on the One Thing**

  - Inspired by the book *The One Thing* by Gary Keller (author was worth hundreds of millions when he wrote it ~20 years ago)
  - Core question: *"What's the one thing that, if you accomplished it, would make everything else easier or unnecessary?"*
  - Work on that one thing for **4 focused hours per day** — no notifications, no distractions
  - Does NOT have to be 4 consecutive hours: can split into 2 hrs morning + 2 hrs evening
  - Does NOT have to be 4 hrs every single day: average 4 hrs/day across the week (miss Friday → do 8 hrs Saturday, or 6 hrs Saturday + 6 hrs Sunday)
  - **Studying and reading do NOT count** toward the 4 hours — must be actually doing the thing
  - Brandon's own one thing: **content creation** — "content makes everything else easier or unnecessary" for online fitness business
  - Promise: "If you put in 4 focused hours a day on your one thing, you will not fail."
  - Common mistake: people spend months on logos, business names, or studying instead of doing revenue-generating activities

- **Rule 3 — Live Out of Your Calendar**

  - "I don't have time" is false; you're just not using time effectively
  - Recommended tool: **Google Calendar only**
  - Two types of tasks:
    - **Recurring tasks**: sleep, wake-up, gym, meals, work hours, commute, family time, date night — lock these in as repeating events
    - **One-time tasks**: vary daily/weekly; schedule these as they come up with no open/empty slots

  - **How to build the calendar (step-by-step example shown live)**
    - Block sleep: e.g., 10 PM – 6 AM, recurring daily (consistent sleep/wake time sets circadian rhythm)
    - Block work: e.g., 9 AM – 5 PM, recurring weekdays
    - Block commute: ~1 hour each way (if commute is over 1 hour, consider moving closer to work)
    - Block gym: 1 hour, recurring daily
    - Block family time / date night (e.g., Tuesdays)
    - Block **"one thing" deep work**: target 28 total hrs/week to average 4 hrs/day
      - Example layout: 2 hrs Wednesday + 2 hrs Tuesday/Friday + 6 hrs Saturday + 6 hrs Sunday ≈ 28 hrs
    - No white space — "white space is the devil"; every hour has a purpose

  - **Side effect: bad habits disappear automatically**
    - If it's not in the calendar, it doesn't happen
    - Brandon quit smoking weed and drinking alcohol 11 years ago — not intentionally, just never scheduled it
    - Won't schedule Call of Duty, doom-scrolling, or time-wasting activities

  - **Addressing common excuses**
    - Kids: use them as motivation, not excuses; go to trampoline parks, do water gun fights, take walks together — all burn calories; wear your Apple Watch during kid activities and track it
    - Girlfriend cooks too much: still your responsibility what you put in your mouth; Brandon's girlfriend weighs his food and cooks to support his goals — but even if she didn't, it's on you
    - "I used to be in shape before kids": don't make your children carry the burden of your failure
